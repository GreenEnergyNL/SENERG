Who we are?

GreenStep is a cutting-edge technology company that transforms the power of footsteps into clean energy. Our innovative flooring tiles generate electricity as people walk, jump or dance on them, making it a fun and interactive way to generate sustainable energy. GreenStep's technology has been used in various public spaces, events, and installations worldwide, making it a game-changer in the field of renewable energy.

Why we use GreenStep Technology?

One reason why people should use GreenStep's technology is because it provides a unique and engaging way to generate renewable energy. By simply walking or moving on GreenStep's flooring tiles, people can actively participate in the production of clean energy, which can contribute to reducing carbon emissions and mitigating climate change.

GreenStep's tiles are durable, easy to install and maintain, and can be customized to fit different design needs, making them a practical and versatile option for public spaces and infrastructure projects.

Why we choose to develop this innovative technology?

GreenStep was founded on the belief that innovation and technology can be harnessed to create positive social and environmental impact. We wanted to find a way to use renewable energy sources to power everyday urban environments, and noticed that the energy generated by footsteps was a vastly untapped resource.

By developing flooring tiles that could harness the kinetic energy from people's footsteps, GreenStep aimed to create a sustainable and interactive way to generate electricity, while also promoting public engagement and awareness of renewable energy solutions. The company's mission is to use innovation and technology to create a more sustainable future for all, and their technology is just one of the ways they are working to achieve this goal.

How does the pavement works step by step:

  • The GreenStep tile is made up of several layers of materials, including a top layer of recycled rubber and a base layer of recycled electronics.
  • When a person steps on the tile, the top layer compresses slightly, causing a series of electromagnetic generators located within the tile to convert the kinetic energy of the footstep into electrical energy.
  • This electrical energy is stored in a small battery located within the tile, which can power a range of devices and applications, including lighting, sensors, and data collection systems.
  • The energy can also be transferred to a larger battery or directly to the grid for use in powering public infrastructure and buildings.
  • GreenStep's technology can be installed in a range of high-traffic public spaces, including transportation hubs, retail centers, and public parks, where it can generate clean energy from foot traffic and engage the public in the transition to more sustainable energy solutions.

Overall, GreenStep's pavement technology is an innovative and practical way to generate sustainable energy from everyday human activity, while also promoting public engagement and awareness of renewable energy solutions.
